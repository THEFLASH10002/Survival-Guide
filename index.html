Essential Research Tools for Computer Science Students

IEEE Xplore

The IEEE Xplore Digital Library is an excellent online resource for those studying electronics, computer science, and electrical engineering. It contains more than five million documents such as books; peer-reviewed journals; conference papers, and standards from IEEE and its associated organizations. This library is an essential availability if you want to study anything new in cybersecurity, machine learning, or AI. Both students and professionals use IEEE Xplore frequently for academic and technical work because it is an original source of reputable research literature. The library is notable for its advanced search capabilities as well as its filtering options and provides an easy way to find the most relevant information about any subject.

Visit IEEE Xplore

ACM Digital Library

The ACM Digital Library is a rich source of literature in the field of computer science and information technology. The Association for Computing Machinery maintains this source, which provides access to thousands of conference proceedings, journal articles, technical magazines, and newsletters, and is especially helpful for understanding cutting-edge research in software engineering, data science, human-computer interaction, and theoretical computing. The library also includes papers from prestigious conferences such as SIGGRAPH, SIGCHI, and SIGPLAN. Overall, this is a great resource for students searching for peer-reviewed, high-impact content that is frequently cited in both educational and industry research literature.

Visit ACM DL

arXiv.org (CS Section)

arXiv is a free-to-access online repository of preprints in mathematics, physics, and computer science. The Computer Science portion of arXiv can provide a great resource for newer - and sometimes even unpublished - research papers on a topic before it is published in a peer-reviewed journal. It includes works in the fields of artificial intelligence, machine learning, computational linguistics, systems design, and more. For authors and researchers, arXiv provides an easy way for them to get their findings out to others, facilitating sharing and collaboration. Although the manuscript is not peer-reviewed, arXiv is an excellent place to monitor trends and new technologies emerging in the field. As such, arXiv is an excellent place for students, researchers, and industry professionals to reference the very latest inventions and practices as they relate to computer science.

Visit arXiv CS

Top Professional Societies for Aspiring Computer Scientists and Engineers

Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)

ACM is one of the largest and most influential computing societies in the world. The society offers its members access to digital libraries, industry publications, networking events, and educational resources. ACM also hosts well-known conferences, including SIGGRAPH and SIGCSE, and offers special interest groups (SIGs) devoted to various subfields of computing such as AI, cybersecurity, and human-computer interaction. An affiliation of the IEEE, the Computer Society concentrates on the promotion of the theory, practice or application of computer and information processing technology. Helping its members succeed in their careers, the IEEE Computer Society provides: access to peer-reviewed technical journals, professional development courses, standards, and conferences. They host some of the most coveted conferences in Computer Science such as the IEEE CS Security and Privacy Symposium and the International Conference on Cloud Computing. They offer reasonable student memberships that include digital library access and job tools. This society is a great way learn from experienced professionals, obtain certifications, and engage in global competitions and technical communities.

Visit IEEE CS

National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE)

NSBE is a vibrant organization dedicated to aiding the academic and professional development of Black engineers and computer scientists. Although it strives to be inclusive, it aims to increase the number of academically successful, professionally skilled, and culturally competent Black engineers who will serve their communities. With more than 600 chapters across the world, NSBE also provides conferences at national and regional levels and hosts career fairs, hackathons, and networking events. Members are provided with scholarships, leadership experiences and opportunities for internships with many companies. NSBE is an invaluable resource dedicated to supporting underrepresented students with a sense of belonging and community in the tech world.

Visit NSBE

NeetCode

NeetCode is a terrific resource for your coding interview preparation, especially for large tech companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon. The site organizes problems similar to LeetCode into sections such as arrays, trees, and dynamic programming. NeetCode has video walkthroughs, cheat sheets, and study plans made for beginners and experienced programmers. NeetCode will help you grasp the underlying logic involved in problem-solving, rather than just making you memorize patterns. NeetCode is great for Computer Science majors looking to refine their data structures and algorithms knowledge for internships jobs and full-time software engineering jobs.

Visit NeetCode

Scholarships, Internships, and Enrichment Opportunities

LinkedIn Jobs

LinkedIn is a networking website, but it is also a job board. You can find internships, full-time jobs, and entry-level tech positions. Many of the listings are posted directly by the recruiters, and you can apply with one easy click, using your LinkedIn profile. You can filter the listings based on location, job type, company, or experience level. This is especially beneficial for computer science students, as companies usually have internship roles posted early in the academic year. Plus, building your LinkedIn profile improves your visibility in front of recruiters in the tech field.

Visit LinkedIn Jobs

Handshake

Handshake is a job and internship portal specifically designed for college students. It connects users with employers seeking recent grads or undergraduate students. Many listings are only available to students at schools that partner with Handshake. You can find internships at startup companies, Fortune 500 companies, and university research labs. Handshake also includes reviews of companies by students, as well as resources for online career fairs and one-on-one meetings with employers. Overall, Handshake is an exciting starting point for students who want to gain work experience before graduating.

Visit Handshake

Scholarships.com (CS)

Scholarships.com maintains an up-to-date listing of scholarships to be awarded specifically to Computer Science majors. The site allows searches by major, background or merit for both private and institutional scholarships. Whether you're a freshman or a master's student, there are scholarships available for your field of study or future career. The site can be a great resource for finding scholarship opportunities that you may never hear about from on-campus resourcesâ€”particularly scholarships for underrepresented students in technology.
